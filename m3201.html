Carbonara Spaghetti

Spaghetti Carbonara, born in post-WWII Rome, combines simplicity with rich flavors, evolving into a global classic through its minimalist ingredients and storied history.

Spaghetti Carbonara today celebrates technique and balance, where creamy texture meets crispy guanciale, offering a timeless dish that embraces tradition while inviting innovation.

Recipe for cabonara spaghetti

preparation
  • Boil salted spaghetti until al dente.
  • Fry diced guanciale (or pancetta) until crisp.
  • Off-heat, toss hot pasta with beaten eggs, cheese, lots of pepper, and a splash of reserved pasta water to create a creamy sauce
ingredients:
  • 2 large eggs and 2 large yolks, room temperature
  • 1ounce (about ⅓ packed cup) grated pecorino Romano, plus additional for serving
  • 1ounce (about ⅓ packed cup) grated Parmesan
  • Coarsely ground black pepper
  • 1tablespoon olive oil
  • 3½ounces of slab guanciale (see recipe), pancetta or bacon, sliced into pieces about ¼ inch thick by ⅓ inch square
  • 12ounces spaghetti (about ¾ box)

xiao long bao

Xiao Long Bao, also known as soup dumplings or Xiaolong mantou, are a type of small, steamed bun filled with a savory soup and minced meat (typically pork). Originating in the Jiangnan region of China, particularly Shanghai and Wuxi, they are known for their thin, delicate wrappers and the flavorful broth that melts from within when steamed.

    ingredient of xiao long bao

  • Ingredients:
  • Dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 300g (10 oz) ground pork
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp white pepper
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 2 tbsp chopped green onions
  • Aspic (pork gelatin): 2 cups pork skin or pork bones
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 slices ginger
  • 2 green onions

    • how to make xiao long bao

  • 1. Make the Aspic (Soup Gelatin) 1.1.Simmer pork skin or bones with water, ginger, and green onions for 2 hours. 1.2.Strain and refrigerate the liquid overnight until it becomes jelly. 1.3.Dice the aspic into small cubes.
  • 2.Make the Dough 2.1.Mix flour and warm water to form a dough. 2.2.Knead for 1 minutes until smooth. 2.3.Cover and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  • 3.Prepare the Filling 3.1.Mix all filling ingredients until sticky. 3.2.Add diced aspic and mix well. 3.3.Chill until ready to wrap.
  • 4.Wrap the Dumplings 4.1.Roll dough into a long log, cut into small pieces. 4.2.Flatten each piece into a 3-inch round wrapper.4.3.Place a spoon of filling with aspic inside.4.4.pleat and pinch the wrapper to close (about 14 pleats).
  • 5.Steam the Dumplings5.1.Line steamer with parchment paper or cabbage leaves.5.2.Steam over boiling water for 9 minutes.
